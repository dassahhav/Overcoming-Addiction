Overcoming Addiction:

A Focus on the Three Most Widely Used Substances in NZ

IMAGE 1

Signs you might be dealing with addiction

  • You keep using it even if it causes problems at school, work, or with friends.
  • You find yourself needing more just to get the same buzz or high
  • You feel like you have to use, even if you don't really want to
  • You tried quitting, yet you can't seem to stick with it
  • You notice your tolerance going up, you need more to feel the same effect
  • Your mood depends on whether you've had it or not
  • You keep using it even when it's making your body or mind feel worse

    What could be the causes of your addiction?

  • Stress overload, because of school, work, family drama, or life just isn't going well.
  • Trying to escape the feeling of being sad, anxious, lonely and using the substances to numb those feelings
  • Low self esteem, using drugs can make you feel more confident or escape insecurity.
  • Being peer pressured by your surroundings, wanting to feel accepted

  • What are the effects over drug overdose?

    Taking drugs might seem like a way to have fun, escape stress, or fit in. But the truth is, a drug overdose can turn your life upside down or even end it in ways you might never expect.

  • You could stop breathing - Overdosing on drugs causes your lungs to shut down, which means no oxygen gets to your brain. Even a few minutes of no oxygen can cause permanent brain damage, or worse death.
  • Your heart can fail - Drugs can cause your heart to beat too fast, too slow, or maybe not at all. When you heart stops, your whole body shuts down too.
  • It can affect your relationships with others - Overdose can affect those around you because it can hurt people who care about you and break their trust
