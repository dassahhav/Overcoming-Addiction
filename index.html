Signs You Might Be Dealing with Addiction

  • You keep using even if it causes problems at school, work, or with friends.
  • You find yourself needing more just to get the same buzz or high.
  • You feel like you have to use, even if you don’t really want to.
  • You tried quitting, yet you can’t seem to stick with it.
  • Your tolerance keeps going up — you need more to feel the same effect.
  • Your mood depends on whether you’ve had it or not.
  • You keep using even when it’s making your body or mind worse.

What Could Be the Causes of Your Addiction?

  • Too much stress: school, work, family drama, or life just not going well.
  • Escaping feelings: sadness, anxiety, or loneliness by numbing emotions.
  • Low self-esteem: using drugs to feel more confident or escape insecurity.
  • Peer pressure: wanting to fit in and feel accepted.

What Are the Effects of Drug Overdose?

Taking drugs might seem like a way to have fun, escape stress, or fit in. But the truth is, a drug overdose can turn your life upside down — or even end it — in ways you might never expect.

  • Severe Physical Damage: your heart, lungs, liver, and brain can stop working properly.
  • Breathing slows down: which can cause brain damage or death within minutes.
  • Emotional and Mental Breakdown: overdosing can trigger severe anxiety, hallucinations, or depression, making life even harder.
